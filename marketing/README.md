## 🎓 Best Practices

### Content Creation

**Do**:
- ✅ Show, don't tell (demos > descriptions)
- ✅ Lead with value proposition
- ✅ Include real cost/time comparisons
- ✅ Use concrete examples
- ✅ Make content scannable (headers, bullets)
- ✅ Include clear CTAs

**Don't**:
- ❌ Oversell or make unrealistic claims
- ❌ Ignore negative feedback
- ❌ Post without testing links
- ❌ Forget to engage in comments
- ❌ Be salesy (focus on value)

### Community Management

**Respond to**:
- Every GitHub issue (within 24 hours)
- Every Product Hunt comment (within 30 minutes on launch day)
- All @mentions on Twitter
- DMs from potential users

**Tone**:
- Helpful and educational
- Transparent about limitations
- Excited but not pushy
- Technical but accessible

### Crisis Management

**If something goes wrong**:
1. Acknowledge quickly (<30 min)
2. Never argue or get defensive
3. Fix the issue if valid
4. Update publicly on resolution
5. Thank for feedback

**Common scenarios**:
- Security concerns → Point to audit, explain
- Feature complaints → Add to roadmap, explain timeline
- Bug reports → Reproduce, fix, update
- Negative reviews → Understand use case, improve docs

## 📊 Template Usage Guide

### How to Use These Templates

1. **Read** the full template first
2. **Customize** with your specific details
3. **Test** on a small audience first
4. **Measure** response and engagement
5. **Iterate** based on results

### Template Variables to Replace

Throughout all templates, replace:
- `[YOUR_NAME]` with your actual name
- `[GITHUB_USERNAME]` with your GitHub username
- `[TWITTER_HANDLE]` with your Twitter handle
- `[DEMO_URL]` with your demo video URL
- `[DISCORD_LINK]` with your Discord invite
- `[EMAIL]` with your contact email

### A/B Testing

Test variations of:
- Headlines (3 versions)
- CTAs (try different wording)
- Opening hooks (problem vs. solution focus)
- Visual styles (screenshots vs. GIFs vs. videos)

Track performance and double down on winners.
